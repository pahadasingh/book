Peter Lynch of Fidelity Magellan mutual-fund fame refers to warnings about the sky falling as “noise,” and we all hear it. Noise is either created inside our heads or comes from outside, often from friends, family, co-workers, and the media. Lynch recalls the time during the 1950s when the threat of nuclear war was so prevalent in the news that people began building fallout shelters and storing food and water. If they had invested that money wisely in the market, instead of building a fallout shelter, they’d probably be ﬁnancially independent today.
